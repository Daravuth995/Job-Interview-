Rising and Falling Intonation Practice

In English, the way your voice rises or falls can completely change how natural and clear you sound. This exercise will help you practice using intonation to speak more fluidly and confidently.

↗️ Rising Intonation (questions, uncertainty)

  • Could you start by introducing yourself? (Rising – Question)
  • Why are you interested in joining our company? (Rising – Question)
  • What would you say are your main strengths? (Rising – Question)
  • Do you have any questions for me? (Rising – Question)

↘️ Falling Intonation (statements, certainty)

  • I have a strong interest in digital marketing and brand strategy. (Falling – Statement)
  • I'm a clear communicator, highly adaptable, and I have excellent time-management skills. (Falling – Statement)
  • I took the initiative to delegate tasks effectively. (Falling – Statement)
  • That sounds ideal. (Falling – Agreement)

Comprehension Questions

  • What is Lina's degree in?
  • Which two areas of marketing is she interested in?
  • What does Lina admire about the company?
  • What are her three main strengths?
  • What is one weakness she mentions?
  • How did Lina solve the problem in her group project?
  • What question does she ask the interviewer?
  • What does the company provide for new employees?
